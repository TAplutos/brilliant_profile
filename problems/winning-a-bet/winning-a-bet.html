Winning a bet

Probability Level 1

Trevor and I were playing golf.

As we all know, Trevor is a pro golfer and I don't know squat about golf (except the fact that we are supposed to hit golf balls with a club).

Now, he made a bet with me. He said, "We will play NNN matches where you're gonna choose the value of NNN. If you win at least one match, I'll pay you $100\$100$100 since I'm super rich and if you don't, you'll pay me $1\$1$1"

Now, I opened my wallet and I saw that it was emptier than a banker's heart. Amongst the cobwebs and flies in it, luckily, there was a $1\$1$1 bill. Naturally, I accepted the bet since I'm almost broke and need cash.

Generally, if I were to play a match against him, I would have a probability of 000 of winning. But since here's money involved, the probability of me winning a single match against him is boosted to 13\dfrac{1}{3}31​.

I want to have at least a 90%90\%90% chance of winning the bet. What is the minimum value of NNN that I should choose?


The answer is 6.

The probability of Trevor winning a match is 2/3. The chance of Parun to win a match can only be atleast 90 percent if the chance of Trevor winning all the match is less than or equal to 10 percent. Let there be N matches than probability of Trevor to win all matches is (2/3)N(2/3)^{N}(2/3)N which should be less than or equal to 0.1.

For this to happen the minimum integer value of N is 6 & the answer is 6

from random import randint
def prob_of_win(N):
    trials = 100000
    wins = 0.0
    for trial in xrange(trials):
        for game in xrange(N):
            # if I won the game
            if randint(1,3) == 1:
                wins += 1
                break
    return wins/trials
N = 1
while prob_of_win(N) < 0.9:
    N += 1
