What is wrong with this proof?

Number Theory Level 1

Consider this "proof" that 0.99=10.99 = 10.99=1:

  1. We know that 13=0.33\frac{1}{3} = 0.3331​=0.33.
  2. Multiplying both sides by 3, we have 33=3(0.33)=0.99\frac{3}{3} = 3(0.33) = 0.9933​=3(0.33)=0.99.
  3. Since 33=1,\frac{3}{3} =1,33​=1, we have that 1=0.99.1 = 0.99.1=0.99.

What is wrong with the proof?

The proof is correct. 3(13) is not equal to 1.3\left(\frac{1}{3}\right) \text{ is not equal to }1.3(31​) is not equal to 1. 13 is not really equal to 0.33.\frac{1}{3} \text{ is not really equal to } 0.33.31​ is not really equal to 0.33. Decimals are not equal to fractions.
