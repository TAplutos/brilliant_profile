A square and two rectangles in a round hole

Calculus Level 4

A square section is cut out of a circle as depicted above. Next, two random rectangles which do not overlap with the other rectangle or the square are cut out of the remaining sectors of the circle. If the maximum possible ratio of the cut out area C to the total area of the circle S is CS\dfrac{C}{S}SC​. Find

⌈(1000CS)⌉\lceil \left(1000\frac{C}{S} \right) \rceil⌈(1000SC​)⌉

Note: this problem was inspired by one of Sir Brian Charlesworth: Rectangular Pegs in a Round Hole

Assume:

Use of a computer software for ARITHMETIC is allowed.

⌈x⌉\lceil x \rceil⌈x⌉ means the least possible integer greater than or equal to x

Exg: ⌈4.1⌉=5\lceil 4.1 \rceil=5⌈4.1⌉=5


The answer is 744.

We'll work assuming that the circle's radius is 1 just to keep our solution pretty.

We begin with making a circle with the equationx2+y2=1x^2+y^2=1x2+y2=1

The inscribed square has a height of 2\sqrt22​, thus the distance from the center to the top is 22\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}22​​.

To make the top side even with the x axis, we must make a vertical shift of 12\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}2​1​ units down and our graph becomes

x2+(y+12)2=1x^2+(y+\frac{1}{\sqrt2})^2=1x2+(y+2​1​)2=1

⇒y=1−x2−12\Rightarrow y=\sqrt{1-x^2}-\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}⇒y=1−x2​−2​1​

Now, looking at the rectangle, we see that the base is along x=0, thus the base length will be 2x2x2x (x is the coordinate of the base along the x-axis).

The height yiy_iyi​ will be corresponding to xix_ixi​. Thus using the formula area=base*height, we get A=(2x)(1−x2−12)A=(2x)(\sqrt{1-x^2}-\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}})A=(2x)(1−x2​−2​1​)

To maximize this, we take the derivative dAdx\dfrac{dA}{dx}dxdA​and set it equal to 0

dAdx=(2x)(1−x2−12)\frac{dA}{dx}=(2x)(\sqrt{1-x^2}-\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}})dxdA​=(2x)(1−x2​−2​1​)

We differentiate in parts

0=(2)(1−x2−12)+(2x)(−x1−x2)0=(2)(\sqrt{1-x^2}-\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}})+(2x)(\dfrac{-x}{\sqrt{1-x^2}})0=(2)(1−x2​−2​1​)+(2x)(1−x2​−x​)

Multiplying out the denominator on both sides and some algebraic manipulation yields 8x4−7x2+1=08x^4-7x^2+1=08x4−7x2+1=0

Using the quadratic then square rooting, we get x=±7−174x=\pm\dfrac{\sqrt{7-\sqrt{17}}}{4}x=±47−17​​​

"Wolfing around" a little with our value of x, plugging it into the equationA=(2x)(1−x2−12)A=(2x)(\sqrt{1-x^2}-\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}})A=(2x)(1−x2​−2​1​).

We get the area of one rectangle to be 18(−214−217+46−217)\frac{1}{8}\left(-2 \sqrt{14-2 \sqrt{17}}+\sqrt{46-2 \sqrt{17}}\right)81​(−214−217​​+46−217​​)

Multiplying this by 2 (there are two rectangles) and adding the area of the cut out square (2 sq units) and finally dividing everything by the area of the circle (π\piπ sq units we get CS=14(−214−217+46−217)+2π≈0.7438\frac{C}{S}=\dfrac{\frac{1}{4} \left(-2 \sqrt{14-2\sqrt{17}}+\sqrt{46-2 \sqrt{17}}\right)+2}{\pi}\approx 0.7438SC​=π41​(−214−217​​+46−217​​)+2​≈0.7438

Therefore ⌈(1000CS)⌉=744\boxed{\lceil \left(1000\frac{C}{S} \right) \rceil=744}⌈(1000SC​)⌉=744​

import random
import sympy
import numpy as np
from sympy import *
#
# 1) Acircle is of radius 1 centered at the origin with the inner square Asquare of area 2
#
# 2) Randomly pic an x in the interval [-sqrt(2)/2, sqrt(2)/2]
#
# 3) Calculate the resulting rectangle
#
Asquare,Acircle=2,np.pi
end,sq22,bigA=10000,(2**.5)/2,-9999
for i in  range(1,end+1):
    sumA=0
    for j in range(1,3):
        x=random.uniform(-sq22,sq22)
        y=sqrt(1-x**2)
        Bheight=y-sq22
        Bwidth=abs(x)*2
        Box=Bheight*Bwidth
        sumA+=Box
    if(sumA>bigA):bigA=sumA
print('++++++++++++++++++')
print('Number Of Loops',end)
Ans=ceiling(1000*(bigA+Asquare)/Acircle)
print(' bigArea, AreaSquare ',bigA,Asquare,'Ratio =',bigA+Asquare)
print('Answer =',Ans)
