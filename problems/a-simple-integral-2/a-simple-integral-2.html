∫−1u3du=12u2+c=sec⁡2(x)2+c.\displaystyle\int -\dfrac{1}{u^{3}} du = \dfrac{1}{2u^{2}} + c = \dfrac{\sec^{2}(x)}{2} + c.∫−u31​du=2u21​+c=2sec2(x)​+c.

This can also be written as tan⁡2(x)+12+c=tan⁡2(x)2+c,\dfrac{\tan^{2}(x) + 1}{2} + c = \dfrac{\tan^{2}(x)}{2} + c,2tan2(x)+1​+c=2tan2(x)​+c, where the "extra" 12\frac{1}{2}21​ term was absorbed by the general constant c.c.c. ∫sec⁡2(x)tan⁡(x)dx\displaystyle \int \sec^2(x) \tan(x) \Bbb{d}x∫sec2(x)tan(x)dx
