Geometry Level 2

'ABC' is a right angled triangle with sides AB=3 cm, BC=4 cm and AC=5 cm. The mid point of 'AC' is marked as 'D'. What is the length 'BD' in cm?


The answer is 2.5.

by Pragjyotish Bhuyan Gogoi, 28, India

Length of the median on the hypotenuse of a right angled triangle is always half the hypotenuse. Just think of Thales theorem!

i.e. the hypotenuse is the diameter of a circle, the median on the hypotenuse is the radius (from the center of the circle - MP of the hypotenuse - to the edge)

The midpoint of ACACAC is the circumcentre of △ABC\triangle ABC△ABC. The length BDBDBD is the radius of the circumcircle of ABC. The radius is also half the distance of AC which is half of 5, which is 2.5.

BD=2(a2+b2)−c22BD=\dfrac{\sqrt{2(a^2+b^2)-c^2}}{2}BD=22(a2+b2)−c2​​

But this is a right triangle, so:

BD=2c2−c22=c2BD=\dfrac{\sqrt{2c^2-c^2}}{2}=\dfrac{c}{2}BD=22c2−c2​​=2c​

We are given c=5c=5c=5, so BD=2.5BD=\boxed{2.5}BD=2.5​.
