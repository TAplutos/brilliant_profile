If θ\thetaθ can be represented by tan⁡−1( a+bc )\tan^{-1}\left( \ \sqrt{\dfrac{a+\sqrt{b}}{c}}\ \right)tan−1( ca+b​​​ ) where bbb is square free and a,b,ca,b,ca,b,c are integers. Find a+b+ca+b+ca+b+c.

Rewrite the equation as

sec⁡2(θ)−1=sec⁡(θ)⟹sec⁡2(θ)−sec⁡(θ)−1=0\sec^{2}(\theta) - 1 = \sec(\theta) \Longrightarrow \sec^{2}(\theta) - \sec(\theta) - 1 = 0sec2(θ)−1=sec(θ)⟹sec2(θ)−sec(θ)−1=0

⟹sec⁡(θ)=1±52\Longrightarrow \sec(\theta) = \dfrac{1 \pm \sqrt{5}}{2}⟹sec(θ)=21±5​​.

Now since we are looking for the smallest positive value of θ\thetaθ we take the positive root for sec⁡(θ)\sec(\theta)sec(θ) and then calculate the corresponding value for tan⁡(θ)\tan(\theta)tan(θ):

tan⁡(θ)=sec⁡2(θ)−1=3+52−1=1+52\tan(\theta) = \sqrt{\sec^{2}(\theta) - 1} = \sqrt{\frac{3 + \sqrt{5}}{2} - 1} = \sqrt{\frac{1 + \sqrt{5}}{2}}tan(θ)=sec2(θ)−1​=23+5​​−1​=21+5​​​

⟹θ=tan⁡−1(1+52)\Longrightarrow \theta = \tan^{-1}(\sqrt{\frac{1 + \sqrt{5}}{2}})⟹θ=tan−1(21+5​​​).

Thus a+b+c=1+5+2=8a + b + c = 1 + 5 + 2 = \boxed{8}a+b+c=1+5+2=8​.
