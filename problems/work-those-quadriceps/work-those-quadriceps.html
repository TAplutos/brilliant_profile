Work those Quadriceps

Geometry Level 5

Suppose ABCDABCDABCD is a cyclic quadrilateral with all integral sidelengths and ADADAD being the circumdiameter. Sides AB‾\overline{AB}AB and BC‾\overline{BC}BC both having length aaa and side CDCDCD having length bbb such that a≠b.a \ne b.a​=b.

Determine the sum of the 4 smallest possible perimeters of quadrilateral ABCD.ABCD.ABCD.

Inspired by this beautiful problem


The answer is 120.

Let OOO be the centre of the circle, MMM be the midpoint of ABABAB, and d=ADd = ADd=AD. Draw the line segments OMOMOM, OBOBOB and OCOCOC. Let θ=∠AOM\theta = \angle AOMθ=∠AOM. Then a=AB=2MA=2OBsin⁡(θ)=dsin⁡(θ)a = AB = 2MA = 2OB \sin(\theta) = d \sin(\theta)a=AB=2MA=2OBsin(θ)=dsin(θ).

By congruent triangles, we can see that ∠AOB=2θ\angle AOB = 2\theta∠AOB=2θ and ∠BOC=2θ\angle BOC = 2\theta∠BOC=2θ, so ∠AOC=4θ\angle AOC = 4\theta∠AOC=4θ. Now Euclid's Elements III.20 says the angle at the circumference is half the angle at the centre (in other words ∠ADC=12∠AOC\angle ADC = \dfrac{1}{2}\angle AOC∠ADC=21​∠AOC). Hence ∠ADC=2θ\angle ADC = 2\theta∠ADC=2θ.

Draw line segment ACACAC. By Thales' theorem, ∠ACD=90∘\angle ACD = 90^{\circ}∠ACD=90∘. Therefore, b=CD=ADcos⁡(2θ)=d(1−2sin⁡2(θ))=d(1−2(ad)2)=d−2a2db = CD = AD \cos(2\theta) = d \left(1 - 2\sin^2(\theta)\right) = d\left(1 - 2\left(\dfrac{a}{d}\right)^2\right) = d - \dfrac{2a^2}{d}b=CD=ADcos(2θ)=d(1−2sin2(θ))=d(1−2(da​)2)=d−d2a2​

Hence 2a22a^22a2 is divisible by ddd. Let's look at a few cases:

a=1→d∣2a = 1 \rightarrow d | 2a=1→d∣2. Now ddd can't be 111 since d>ad > ad>a. It also can't be 222 because then we would have b=ab = ab=a. Hence there are no solutions in this case.

a=2→d∣8a = 2 \rightarrow d | 8a=2→d∣8. Now d=1,2d = 1,2d=1,2 is too small, and d=4d = 4d=4 leads to a=ba = ba=b. However d=8d = 8d=8 works, giving a solution of (a,b,d)=(2,7,8)(a,b,d) = (2,7,8)(a,b,d)=(2,7,8).

a=3→d∣18a = 3 \rightarrow d | 18a=3→d∣18. Here d=1,2,3d = 1,2,3d=1,2,3 are too small and d=6d = 6d=6 leads to a=ba = ba=b. However d=9,18d = 9, 18d=9,18 work, giving solutions of (3,7,9)(3,7,9)(3,7,9) and (3,17,18)(3,17,18)(3,17,18).

a=4→d∣32a = 4 \rightarrow d | 32a=4→d∣32. Here d=1,2,4d = 1,2,4d=1,2,4 are too small and d=8d = 8d=8 leads to a=ba = ba=b. However, d=16,32d = 16,32d=16,32 give the solutions (4,14,16)(4,14,16)(4,14,16) and (4,31,32)(4, 31, 32)(4,31,32).

So far the 4 smallest perimeters are 19,22,38,4119, 22, 38, 4119,22,38,41. All that's left to do is prove that these are the smallest possible perimeters. However, this is a little tedious and 2 other people have already done it, so I'll skip this part.

Hence the answer is 19+22+38+41=12019+22+38+41 = \boxed{120}19+22+38+41=120​.
