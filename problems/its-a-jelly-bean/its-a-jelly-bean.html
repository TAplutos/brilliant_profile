The graph above shows the figure created by the function x2+(y+x)2=1x^2+(y+x)^2=1x2+(y+x)2=1.

Find the area of this tilted ellipse correct to 2 decimal places.


The answer is 3.14.

All we need to do is to rotate the co-ordinate axes so that they coincide with the major and minor axes of the ellipse. This will render the ellipse in the standard form. But the beauty is that we don't even need to do all the calculations explicitly if we employ a tiny bit of linear algebra. First, notice that x2+(y+x)2=2x2+2xy+y2=z⃗TAz⃗x^2+(y+x)^2=2x^2+2xy+y^2=\vec{z}^{T}A\vec{z}x2+(y+x)2=2x2+2xy+y2=zTAz where A=(2111)A=\begin{pmatrix} 2 &1 \\ 1 &1 \end{pmatrix}A=(21​11​) and z⃗=(xy)\vec{z}=\begin{pmatrix}x \\ y \end{pmatrix}z=(xy​). Now let us write the eigen decomposition of AAA as A=UΛUTA=U\Lambda U^{T}A=UΛUT where UUU is an orthonormal (rotation) matrix and Λ\LambdaΛ is a diagonal matrix consisting of eigenvalues of AAA. Since rotation does not change the area, we change the co-ordinates as follows ζ⃗=UTz⃗\vec{\zeta}=U^T\vec{z}ζ​=UTz. With this transformation, the equation of the ellipse becomes ζ⃗TΛζ⃗=1\vec{\zeta}^{T}\Lambda\vec{\zeta}=1ζ​TΛζ​=1 However, since Λ\LambdaΛ is diagonal, the above is equivalent to λ1ζ12+λ2ζ22=1\lambda_1\zeta_1^2+\lambda_2\zeta_2^2=1λ1​ζ12​+λ2​ζ22​=1 This is an ellipse in standard form and its area SSS is well-known to be S=πλ1λ2=πdet(A)S=\frac{\pi}{\sqrt{\lambda_1\lambda_2}}=\frac{\pi}{\sqrt{\text{det}(A)}}S=λ1​λ2​​π​=det(A)​π​ Thus all we need to do is to compute det(A)\text{det}(A)det(A), which is 111 in this case and the answer follows.
