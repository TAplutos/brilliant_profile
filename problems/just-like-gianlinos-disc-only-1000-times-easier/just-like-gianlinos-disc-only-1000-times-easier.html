Geometry Level 5

Points AAA and BBB are placed at (2,0)(2,0)(2,0) and (−2,0)(-2,0)(−2,0) respectively. A circle of radius 3 is then drawn centered at the origin.

Next, a point EEE is randomly placed on the circumference of circle C centered at the origin with radius 1.

If the probability that the circumcenter of △ABE\triangle ABE△ABE lies within the circle of radius 3 can be represented by mn\dfrac{m}{n}nm​ where m,nm,nm,n are coprime positive integers, find m+n.


The answer is 5.

First thing to note, is that the circumcenter is situated on the y-axis, regardless of where point EEE is at. This is so because the circumcenter is a point which the perpendicular bisectors of ABABAB, AEAEAE and EBEBEB will intersect, and the perpendicular bisector of ABABAB would just be the line x=0x=0x=0.

Due to symmetry, we just have to focus on the first quadrant of the circle. Let OOO be the origin, and ∠EOA=a\angle EOA=a∠EOA=a. Now, when aaa is smaller than a certain value k°k°k°, the circumcenter will be out of the circle of radius 3. When aaa is bigger however, but not more than 90°, the circumcenter will be within the circle. So what we need to do is find that value of kkk. To do that, we make the circumcenter situate at (0,3)(0,3)(0,3)

From the fact that the (black) circle's center is (0,3)(0,3)(0,3), and it passes through (2,0)(2,0)(2,0), we can find, through Pythagoras theorem, the radius of the circle as 13\sqrt{13}13​. Redrawing the diagram, we get

Through this, we can find the value of kkk using the cosine rule, which happens to be 303030.

Therefore, if 0°<∠EOA<30°0°<\angle EOA < 30°0°<∠EOA<30°, the circumcenter would be out of the circle of radius 333. If 90°>∠EOA>30°90°>\angle EOA > 30°90°>∠EOA>30°, the circumcenter would be in the circle.

Hence, the probability that the circumcenter is in the circle or radius 333 is 90−3090=23\frac{90-30}{90}=\frac{2}{3}9090−30​=32​
