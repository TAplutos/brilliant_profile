Center of gravity

Classical Mechanics Level 4

A 3×3×33\times 3\times 33×3×3 cube resting on the ground has a 2×2×22\times 2\times 22×2×2 cube cut out of its center leaving each side of the cube with thickness .5.5.5 (except for the corners). It's hollow center is then filled with water so that the center of mass of the cube is as close to the ground as possible.

If the shortest distance from the ground to the center of mass of the water filled cube is MMM where MMM is in meters, find ⌊100M⌋\lfloor 100M \rfloor⌊100M⌋.

Important details:

The hollow part of the cube that is not filled with water is filled with helium gas. (Hint: you don't need to account for the helium gas, it changes the height of the center of mass by less than 0.0006 m)

All measurements are given in meters.

Density of the cube is 500 Kgm3500~\frac{Kg}{m^3}500 m3Kg​

Density of water is 1000 Kgm31000 ~ \frac{Kg}{m^3}1000 m3Kg​

You may use Wolfram Alpha


The answer is 134.
