We do the normal method and change the equation to

x+y=y\sqrt{x+y}=yx+y​=y

Next, we remove the radical and put everything on one side of the equation.

y2−y−x=0y^2-y-x=0y2−y−x=0

For this to be factorable and because y is dependent upon x,x has to be able to be written as a(a+1)a(a+1)a(a+1). Implying that it can be factored into

(y+a)(y−(a+1))⇒y2−y(a+1)+ya−a(a+1)⇒y2−y−a(a+1)(y+a)(y-(a+1))\Rightarrow y^2-y(a+1)+ya-a(a+1)\Rightarrow y^2-y-a(a+1)(y+a)(y−(a+1))⇒y2−y(a+1)+ya−a(a+1)⇒y2−y−a(a+1)

Thus we need to plug in a=[1,2,3...9]a=[1,2,3...9]a=[1,2,3...9] since a=10 implies x>100x>100x>100

After plugging and chugging, we find our answer to be 330.
