Binary less than 100

Number Theory Level 3

Find the number of integers that are between 111 and 100100100 inclusive that when converted to binary have a digit sum of less than 5.5.5.


The answer is 87.

We will prove our answer by looking at the complement.

Looking at binary, we have the largest value less than 100 to be 110010011001001100100

There will be (75)+(76)+(77)=29\dbinom{7}{5}+\dbinom{7}{6}+\dbinom{7}{7}=29(57​)+(67​)+(77​)=29 numbers that have a digit sum greater than 4.

Now we have to look at numbers that exceed 100 but have a digit sum less than 4

There will be 4 numbers that are less then 100 and have a digit sum less than 4 (1100100, 1100011, 1100001, 1100010), so we'll put these aside for now.

Looking at the numbers, we have (51)+(52)=15\dbinom{5}{1}+\dbinom{5}{2}=15(15​)+(25​)=15

However, we already decided that 4 will work, so we subtract 4, 15−4=1115-4=1115−4=11

Totaling our numbers that don't work we have 11+29=40

Since we have 127 numbers between 127 and 1, and 40 that don't work, we have

127−40=87127-40=87127−40=87 numbers that work.
