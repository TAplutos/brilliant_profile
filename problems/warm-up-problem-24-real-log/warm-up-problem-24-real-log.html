Thus \ln(\ln(x)), \ln(x)=0\Longrightarrow x=e^{\color \green{0}}=\color \red{1}

\ln(\ln(\ln(x)))\Longrightarrow \ln(\ln(x))=0 \Longrightarrow \ln(x)=1 \Longrightarrow x=e^{\color \red{1}}=e

Notice the pattern: e to the power of the answer to the previous line (shown by the colors). When there are 3 natural logs. We have x=ex=ex=e. When there are 4, x=eex=e^ex=ee
