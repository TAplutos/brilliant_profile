Number Theory Level 2

N=7p+4×5q×23\Large N=7^{p+4}\times 5^q \times 2^3 N=7p+4×5q×23

If NNN is a perfect cube where p,q∈Np,q \in \mathbb{N}p,q∈N , find the smallest possible value of p+qp + qp+q.

This question is a part of JEE Novices.


The answer is 5.

each number is a prime, so it must be expressed as nan^ana where a is an integer. If it's not, we won't have a perfect cube. Each number's power must be a multiple of 3, so that N will be a perfect cube.

Thus 3∣p+4⇒p=23|p+4\Rightarrow p=23∣p+4⇒p=2

3∣q⇒q=33|q\Rightarrow q=33∣q⇒q=3

Since N is a perfect cube hence N must be a product of cubes of its prime factors. Moreover, since p and q are natural numbers, therefore p>0 and q>0.

We know 7, 5 and 2 are primes hence their exponents must be multiples of 3
Therefore, Minimum value of p = 2 and that of q =3 Hence p+q = 5
