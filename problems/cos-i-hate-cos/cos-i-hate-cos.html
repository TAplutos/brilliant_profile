Been a while since I used Chebyshev polynomials, so here we go.

We're working with CP of the first kind. Ignore the 60 for now so we have cos⁡(20)cos⁡(40)cos⁡(80)\cos(20)\cos(40)\cos(80)cos(20)cos(40)cos(80). Our polynomial will be of degree three and have roots of cos⁡(3θ)\cos(3\theta)cos(3θ). The first coefficient is 2 to the power of the degree of the equation-1.

22x3+...+0=cos⁡(3⋅20)2^2x^3+...+0=\cos(3\cdot20)22x3+...+0=cos(3⋅20)

4x3+...−1/2=04x^3+...-1/2=04x3+...−1/2=0

The product of this polynomial's roots is the negation of the last coefficient divided by the first.

−−1/24=18-\dfrac{-1/2}{4}=\dfrac{1}{8}−4−1/2​=81​. Multiplying this by cos⁡(60)\cos(60)cos(60) we have our answer 116\dfrac{1}{16}161​
