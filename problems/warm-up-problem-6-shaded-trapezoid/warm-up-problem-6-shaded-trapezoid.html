Interesting Trapezoid

Geometry Level 2

The above figure shows five congruent rectangles. A trapezoid is drawn in such a way that its vertices coincide with vertices of some of the rectangles. What percentage of the figure is shaded?

35% 30% 20% 40%

Looking at all these complex formulas... makes me sad. I got next to zero formulas left in my old head, so I solved it like this.

  1. since all rectangles are equal, then each rectangle is 20% of total area.
  2. the top-left white triangle is half of 4 rectangles, i.e. 80% / 2 = 40%
  3. the bottom-right white triangle is half of 3 rectangles, i.e. 60% / 2 = 30%
  4. all white area is 70%, so the difference is 30%.

There are 5 same area rectangles. Assume each rectangle's area is 1 unit. Then, total area is 5 * 1 = 5(unit). The area of above triangle (bigger one) is a half of 4 rectangles' area = (1/2) * 4 * 1 = 2 (unit). The area of bottom triangle (smaller one) is a half of 3 rectangles' area = (1/2) * 3 * 1 =1.5 (unit). Therefore, the area of trapezoid is 5 - 2 - 1.5 = 1.5 (unit). The percentage of the shaded figure = 1.5/5 = 30%

This is how I solved this. Let's name the pieces in the upper half (not actually half) as A,B,C and D and the pieces in the lower half as E,F and G. All the pieces are named from left to right.

It is clear that A,D and B,C pairs of pieces form one rectangle each which sums to 2 rectangles. In the lower part it is clear that the 3 rectangles have been cut by a diagonal, so that's 3/2=1.5 rectangles. So the unshaded region is 2+1.5=3.5 which means the shaded region is 5-3.5=1.5 rectangles. SO the percentage of shaded region is (1.5/5)*100=30%.

There you go with pure logic and zero formulae.
