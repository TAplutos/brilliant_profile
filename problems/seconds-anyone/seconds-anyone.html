Geometry Level 4

A concentric circle, (radius 23\dfrac{2}{\sqrt{3}}3​2​), and square, (side length 222), have a region of overlap with an area of

abb+acπ\dfrac{a}{b} \sqrt{b} + \dfrac{a}{c} \piba​b​+ca​π,

where aaa is coprime with both bbb and ccc. Find a+b+ca + b + ca+b+c.


The answer is 16.

Looking at the circle, we can observe that the distance from the center to point of intersection is 23\frac{2}{\sqrt3}3​2​ and the distance o the mid point is 1. This forms a 30-60-90 triangle with the 30 degree angle being the one closest to the center.

Summing the area of these for triangles, we get 4×(13)=4334\times \left(\dfrac{1}{\sqrt3}\right)=\dfrac{4\sqrt3}{3}4×(3​1​)=343​​

Looking at the sum of central triangle areas, the remaining degree of measure is 120, thus it is 1/3 the area of the circle. π3(23)2=4π9\dfrac{\pi}{3}\left(\dfrac{2}{\sqrt3}\right)^2=\dfrac{4\pi}{9}3π​(3​2​)2=94π​.

Thus summing our results, we get 433+4π9\dfrac{4\sqrt3}{3}+\dfrac{4\pi}{9}343​​+94π​. thus a+b+c=16\boxed{16}16​
