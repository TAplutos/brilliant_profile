IMO Day 1 Problem 1: Vector/trig bash

I almost forgot how fun challenging math was until my plane ride last week where I solved IMO problem 1. Then I fell right back in love with math again. Anyway, let's begin. (Please excuse my notations and terminology, I'm a bit rusty on that).

Problem:

Triangle BCFBCFBCF has a right angle at BBB. Let AAA be the point on line CFCFCF such that FA=FBFA = FBFA=FB and FFF lies between AAA and CCC. Point DDD is chosen so that DA=DCDA = DCDA=DC and ACACAC is the bisector of ∠DAB\angle{DAB}∠DAB. Point EEE is chosen so that EA=EDEA = EDEA=ED and ADADAD is the bisector of ∠EAC\angle{EAC}∠EAC. Let MMM be the midpoint of CFCFCF. Let XXX be the point such that AMXEAMXEAMXE is a parallelogram. Prove that BDBDBD, FXFXFX, and MEMEME are concurrent.

Solution

Let BC‾=a, and FB‾=bi\overline{BC}=a,~\text{and}~\overline{FB}=biBC=a, and FB=bi

We are given

C=⟨a,0⟩B=⟨0,0⟩M=⟨a2,b2⟩F=⟨0,b⟩\boxed{\begin{aligned} C &= \langle a,0 \rangle\\ B &= \langle 0,0 \rangle\\ M &= \langle \frac{a}{2},\frac{b}{2} \rangle \\ F &= \langle 0,b \rangle \end{aligned}}CBMF​=⟨a,0⟩=⟨0,0⟩=⟨2a​,2b​⟩=⟨0,b⟩​​

Now let angle ∠JAB=θ\angle \text{JAB}=\theta∠JAB=θ and the rest are labeled in the picture for you. (IGNORE POINT G). Also, define CF‾=a2+b2=r\overline{CF}=\sqrt{a^2+b^2}=rCF=a2+b2​=r.

Now define PxP_xPx​ as the xxx-coordinate of point PPP and define PyP_yPy​ similarly. Segments BF‾\overline{BF}BF and FA‾\overline{FA}FA are both length bbb. Thus Cx⋅br=AxC_x\cdot \dfrac{b}{r}=A_xCx​⋅rb​=Ax​ and Fy⋅b+rr=AyF_y\cdot \dfrac{b+r}{r}=A_yFy​⋅rb+r​=Ay​.

Some arithmetic yields A=⟨−abr,b2r+b⟩\boxed{A= \langle -\frac{ab}{r},\frac{b^2}{r}+b \rangle}A=⟨−rab​,rb2​+b⟩​

Next, it is obvious that since CA‾=b+r\overline{CA}=b+rCA=b+r, segment DC‾=AD‾=b+r2cos⁡(θ)\overline{DC}=\overline{AD}=\dfrac{b+r}{2\cos(\theta)}DC=AD=2cos(θ)b+r​.

And it follows

D=C⃗+b+r2cos⁡(θ)⋅cis(π2)D=\vec{C}+\frac{b+r}{2\cos(\theta)}\cdot \text{cis}\left(\dfrac{\pi}{2}\right)D=C+2cos(θ)b+r​⋅cis(2π​)

D=⟨a−b+r2tan⁡(θ),b+r2⟩D= \langle a-\frac{b+r}{2}\tan(\theta), \frac{b+r}{2} \rangleD=⟨a−2b+r​tan(θ),2b+r​⟩

Similar triangles tells us that JB‾=abr\overline{JB}=\frac{ab}{r}JB=rab​ and point AAA tells us that JA‾=b2r+b\overline{JA}=\frac{b^2}{r}+bJA=rb2​+b.

Thus the picture shows us that tan⁡θ=ab+r\tan{\theta}=\frac{a}{b+r}tanθ=b+ra​

Amazingly, this means that point DDD is directly over point MMM with coordinates D=⟨a2,b+r2⟩\boxed{D= \langle \frac{a}{2}, \frac{b+r}{2} \rangle}D=⟨2a​,2b+r​⟩​

Next, it is apparent that AE‾=ED‾=AD‾2cos⁡(θ)=b+r4cos⁡2(θ)\overline{AE}=\overline{ED}=\dfrac{\overline{AD}}{2\cos(\theta)}=\dfrac{b+r}{4\cos^2(\theta)}AE=ED=2cos(θ)AD​=4cos2(θ)b+r​.

Pythagorean theorem tells us that AB‾=b2+2br\overline{AB}=b\sqrt{2+\frac{2b}{r}}AB=b2+r2b​​. Unbelievably, this is pretty much the only root we have to deal with besides rrr in all of this problem. And this root goes away immediately. Props to the maker of this beautiful problem.

From △ABJ\triangle ABJ△ABJ we know what cos⁡2(θ)\cos^2(\theta)cos2(θ) is and thus AE‾=b+r4cos⁡2(θ)=r2\overline{AE}=\dfrac{b+r}{4\cos^2(\theta)}=\dfrac{r}{2}AE=4cos2(θ)b+r​=2r​.

Thus E=A⃗+cis(3π2+r2)E=\vec{A}+\text{cis}\left(\frac{3\pi}{2}+\frac{r}{2}\right)E=A+cis(23π​+2r​)

Not surprisingly, everything cancels once again and we are left with

E=⟨0,b+r2⟩\boxed{E= \langle 0,b+\frac{r}{2} \rangle}E=⟨0,b+2r​⟩​

Finally,

X⃗=M⃗−A⃗+E⃗\vec{X} =\vec{M}-\vec{A}+\vec{E}X=M−A+E

X=⟨a2+abr,b+r2−b2r⟩\boxed{X =\langle \frac{a}{2}+\frac{ab}{r}, \frac{b+r}{2} -\frac{b^2}{r} \rangle}X=⟨2a​+rab​,2b+r​−rb2​⟩​

We now have

C=⟨a,0⟩B=⟨0,0⟩M=⟨a2,b2⟩F=⟨0,b⟩A=⟨−abr,b2r+b⟩D=⟨a2,b+r2⟩E=⟨0,b+r2⟩X=⟨a2+abr,b+r2−b2r⟩\boxed{\begin{aligned} C &= \langle a,0 \rangle\\ B &= \langle 0,0 \rangle\\ M &= \langle \frac{a}{2},\frac{b}{2} \rangle \\ F &= \langle 0,b \rangle \\ A &= \langle -\frac{ab}{r},\frac{b^2}{r}+b \rangle \\ D &= \langle \frac{a}{2}, \frac{b+r}{2} \rangle \\ E &= \langle 0,b+\frac{r}{2} \rangle \\ X &=\langle \frac{a}{2}+\frac{ab}{r}, \frac{b+r}{2} -\frac{b^2}{r} \rangle \end{aligned}}CBMFADEX​=⟨a,0⟩=⟨0,0⟩=⟨2a​,2b​⟩=⟨0,b⟩=⟨−rab​,rb2​+b⟩=⟨2a​,2b+r​⟩=⟨0,b+2r​⟩=⟨2a​+rab​,2b+r​−rb2​⟩​​

I didn't take the time to actually work out the lines in variable form. But plugging in values for a and b show that the three lines are concurrent.

I unfortunately lost the paper that I had the coordinates of intersection on. But the coordinates are sooo pretty, no radicals, just simple, elegant fractions.
