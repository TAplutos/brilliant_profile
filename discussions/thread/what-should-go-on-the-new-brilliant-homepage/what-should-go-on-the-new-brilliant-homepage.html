What should go on the new Brilliant homepage?

Hey everyone, we came up with a new layout for Brilliant, to leave more room on the homepage for a dashboard to help you quickly get to different parts of the site.

Currently, the homepage is very minimal – we only show you your topics and some suggested practice sections. You can help us decide what to add next by telling us what you would like to see. Here are a few ideas:

  • A preview of the 5 best new problems and notes since your last visit, with a link to see all new posts from the community
  • A featured problem of the day (Mondays are easiest, Sundays are hardest)
  • A featured wiki page of the day
  • If you're a contributor to Brilliant, stats on your posts from the past week
  • Your level in every topic
  • A leaderboard preview with the most recently active people you follow, pointing to the full leaderboard
  • Notifications about new comments or solutions on problems or notes that you recently viewed
  • Links to wiki pages for recent practice sections you visited, in addition to links to the problem sets (which already exist on the new homepage)
  • Links to drafts of wiki pages, problems, notes, or solutions you were recently writing (which includes the ability to save drafts)
  • A preview of the 5 most recent items you saved to a set, with a link to take you directly to all of your sets

Let us know which ones are your favorites, or feel free to make suggestions that are not already on this list. Any of these features will take us a while to implement, so we'd like to get a sense of what you'd like to see the most, to inform what we work on next.

Thanks in advance for your feedback!

P.S. If you would also like to comment on how we can improve the Community tab, you can do so in this thread.

  Easy Math Editor

This discussion board is a place to discuss our Daily Challenges and the math and science related to those challenges. Explanations are more than just a solution — they should explain the steps and thinking strategies that you used to obtain the solution. Comments should further the discussion of math and science.

When posting on Brilliant:

  • Use the emojis to react to an explanation, whether you're congratulating a job well done , or just really confused .
  • Ask specific questions about the challenge or the steps in somebody's explanation. Well-posed questions can add a lot to the discussion, but posting "I don't understand!" doesn't help anyone.
  • Try to contribute something new to the discussion, whether it is an extension, generalization or other idea related to the challenge.
  • Stay on topic — we're all here to learn more about math and science, not to hear about your favorite get-rich-quick scheme or current world events.
